Heat Capacity of Water

We are going to calculate the heat capacity of water. To calculate Cp we need to run a constant pressure simulation. To calculate Cv we need to run a constant volume simulation. These folders have been set up and are called waterboxNPT and waterboxNVT, respectively.

  1. Navigate into each of these directories and submit the jobs.
  2. Once the simulations have been completed, use the .sh scripts provided to extract total energy and total potential energy. These will allow you to calculate the Cp and Cv:

    3. $$C_v=\frac{\langle E^2 \rangle-\langle E \rangle^2}{k_BT^2}$$
    $$C_p=\frac{\langle U^2 \rangle-\langle U \rangle^2}{k_BT^2}$$
    where E is the total energy and U is the potential energy.
  3. What is the relationship between Cp and Cv? (This is google-able.) Do these results agree with this relationship?
Radial Distribution Functions and Root-Mean-Square Deviation of Solvated TRP-Cage

We are also going to simulate a small protein called the TRP-Cage. The necessary files have been provided in the directory TRP-Cage

  1. Run the job, transfer the data to your local computer, and load the trajectory into VMD. (You should be getting good at this now!)
  2. To make things easier to visualize go to “Graphics → Representations”
  3. For selected atoms type ions. Change this representation’s drawing method to VDW.
  4. Click Create Rep and for selected atoms type Backbone. Change this representation’s drawing method to Ribbon.
  5. Click Create Rep and for selected atoms type Water. Change this representation’s drawing method to Line.
  6. A way to track if a simulation has equilibrated is to look at how much the structure of the protein has changed since the beginning of the simulation. This is done by aligning the center of mass for the protein and calculating the root mean squared displacement (RMSD) of each backbone atom. Usually the RMSD rise quickly at the start of the simulation as a protein relaxes and plateaus once the system is equilibrated.
  7. Go to analysis in VMD and select the “RMSD trajectory” tool. Click align and calculate. Then save the data using the drop down in the top left. Plot this in excel.
  8. Now we get to see the real power of RDFs. Calculate 2 RDFs: the first between ions and type OT, and the second between backbone and type OT. The first is the structure of water around the ions and the second around the protein.

Molecular Orbitals

And now for something completely different. In this project, we will investigate the molecular orbitals and molecular orbital energies of the homonuclear diatomic molecules N2 and O2 in both ground and excited states. We will construct molecular orbital energy diagrams and will compare and contrast their structures, orbitals, and energies. In addition, we will characterize the molecular orbitals as σ bonding or antibonding, π bonding or antibonding.

Construction of MO energy diagrams of N2 and O2
  1. Sketch the MO energy diagrams of N2 and O2. Make sure to label and fill the atomic orbitals as well as the molecular orbitals. Be careful of the 1π and 3σ orbitals! they switch order between N2 and O2. Identify each orbital as gerade or ungerade symmetry.
  2. Report the multiplicities of N2 and O2 that are predicted by your MO diagrams
  3. Compute and report the bond order of N2 and N2 from the MO energy diagram.
Geometry Optimization and Energy Calculations of N2 and N2
  1. Using Avogadro, build a N2 molecule. Minimize the energy within Avogadro to get a reasonable starting bond length
  2. Using the Extensions -> Gaussian input generator, generate a Gaussian input file named n2-singlet.com with the following specifications:
    • Calculation: Geometry Optimization
    • Theory: RHF
    • Basis: 6-31G(d)
    • Charge: 0
    • Multiplicity: 1
    • Make sure the Checkpoint button is clicked! (this enables viewing of the molecular orbitals)
  3. After you generate the input file, you will need to edit the type of job that it is running.
    • Change the route section line to read
      #n RHF/6-31G(d) Opt Pop=Full
    • Then make sure that there is a line that reads
      %Chk=n2-singlet.chk
      right above it.
  4. Move the file to the directory on your computer named lab3/n2-singlet/ and run the job using the .pbs script.
  5. The results you need for the singlet N2 molecule include the equilibrium bond length and electronic energy.
    • Measure the bond length with the measure tool within Avogadro
    • The electronic energies will be found in the .log file grep the log file for "SCF Done" to find the energy (in Hartrees)
  6. Repeat these steps for triplet N2, singlet O2, and triplet O2
    • Note: For the singlet states use RHF (restricted Hartree Fock) as the method
    • For each of the triplet states use UHF (unrestricted Hartree Fock) as the method
Classification of Molecular Orbitals of N2 and O2
  1. To view the MO orbitals of our molecules, we need to convert the format of our formatted checkpoint file. Navigate to the n2-singlet/ directory and enter the following command: 
    formchk -3 n2-singlet.chk n2-singlet.FChk
  2. Transfer the n2-singlet.FChk file to your Desktop and open it with Avogadro.
  3. The list of molecular orbitals should appear to the right of the molecule in the Avogadro window. Click on the listing for the HOMO in the window. The highest occupied molecular orbital should be displayed.
  4. Under Surfaces in the display types list on the left of the screen, you can play with the settings: surface type, color, opacity. Find something pretty and save a bunch of pictures!
  5. Repeat the process for triplet O2
